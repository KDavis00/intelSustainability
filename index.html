Renewable Energy

Intel has made significant strides in renewable energy adoption, powering its operations with 100% renewable electricity. This commitment has led to Intel becoming one of the largest corporate purchasers of green power in the United States.

Water Conservation

Through innovative water management practices and recycling programs, Intel has saved billions of gallons of water. The company implements cutting-edge water conservation technologies in its manufacturing processes.

Emissions Reduction

Intel's comprehensive approach to reducing greenhouse gas emissions includes energy-efficient manufacturing, green building design, and investment in carbon offset projects worldwide.

Sustainable Facilities

Intel's facilities are designed with sustainability in mind, featuring LEED certification, smart building technologies, and innovative waste management systems that maximize resource efficiency.

1960s

The dawn of computing, with foundational innovations that would later enable energy efficiency.

1968

Intel Founded

Intel was founded by Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore to pursue bold ideas in computing.

1969

Intel's First Product

Intel released its first product, the 3101 Schottky RAM, generating revenue while engineers developed metal-oxide semiconductors.

The 1101

Intel released the 1101 static RAM, the first commercial chip using metal-oxide semiconductors and silicon gates. These innovations improved efficiency and cost, paving the way for PCs.

1970s

Intel grew into a leading tech company, introducing the first programmable microprocessor and making computing affordable.

1971

First Company-Owned Facility

In 1971, Intel opened its first company-owned manufacturing facility in Santa Clara, California.

First Microprocessor

Intel debuts the 4004, the world's first commercial microprocessor.

1972

Introduction of the 1103 DRAM

Intel introduced the 1103, the world's first commercially available dynamic RAM (DRAM) chip.

1973-1975

Expansion and Technological Advancements

Intel expanded its product line and manufacturing capabilities while focusing on key technological advancements.

1976

Introduction of the 8080

Intel introduced the 8080 microprocessor, which became the foundation for many early personal computers.

1978

8086 Processor

Intel released the 8086 microprocessor, a 16-bit processor that enabled more efficient computing.

1980s

Intel expanded globally; environmental considerations were minimal but energy and resource tracking began informally.

1981-1983

Energy Management and the 386 Processor

Intel refined manufacturing processes to reduce waste and optimize energy use.

1985

Exit from DRAM Market

Intel exited the DRAM market and focused resources on microprocessors, leading to the 386 processor.

386 Processor

The 386 used over 275,000 transistors. Improved yields reduced waste of raw materials, water, and energy.

1988

Environmental Initiatives Begin

Intel implemented a "smoke-free" policy, showing early awareness of corporate responsibility.

2000s

The company intensifies its focus on environmental stewardship and corporate social responsibility.

2000

First Public Report

Intel published its first Corporate Responsibility Report on environmental performance.

2006

Peak GHG Emissions

Intel reached its highest annual greenhouse gas emissions and began reductions.

2010s

Renewable energy commitments and responsible sourcing became top priorities for Intel.

2010

New Goals

Intel set a goal to source 100% renewable electricity by 2030 and invested in water conservation.

2012

Conflict-Free Minerals

Intel declared its microprocessors conflict-free, a milestone in supply chain ethics.

Renewable Energy Leadership

Intel was the largest voluntary U.S. corporate purchaser of renewable energy, using over 2.5B kWh annually.

2014

Water Recycling

Intel implemented advanced water conservation measures, reaching near-100% recycling in some sites.

2020s

Intel set ambitious long-term goals for carbon neutrality and sustainable operations.

2020

RISE Strategy

Intel launched its RISE strategy focused on climate, water, and waste sustainability.

2022

Net-Zero By 2040

Intel pledged to achieve net-zero Scope 1 and 2 emissions by 2040, and Scope 3 by 2050.

2023

Renewable Electricity

Intel reached 99% renewable electricity use worldwide, on track for 100% by 2030.

2024 and onward

Sustainability Summit

Intel hosted its first Sustainability Summit, uniting industry leaders for sustainable semiconductor manufacturing.

To the future

Intel continues progress toward 2030 goals, including net positive water in key regions and zero waste to landfill targets.

Environment

Intel is dedicated to reducing greenhouse gas emissions, conserving water, and reaching net-zero goals by 2040.
